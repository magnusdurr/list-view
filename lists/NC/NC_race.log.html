General Necron changes

The Necrons has undergone major changes compared to the NetEA and Epic UK lists. We felt that the Necron list played too differently from all other Epic armies, and were breaking some of the most basic rules for how armies behave in Epic. This is our attempt at bringing the Necron army closer to how other armies work, while still keeping the unique way Necron operate with their teleporting portals.

The phase out special rule has been changed radically. Formations no longer phase out when broken. Instead they phase out when the formation breaks and is under half strength. Once phased out the formation counts as destroyed and will not return to the battle. This change has two important implications:

  • The portals will become much more predictable. The Necron player still has great flexibility when deploying his portals, but has to think harder on where he wants them, as they are slow to reposition. When playing against the Necrons you now have better control and chance to react as you, after the initial teleport, know roughly where the portals will operate. And hopefully you will no longer end up in a wierd situation where you rather not break the opponents portals as this will most likely cause them to reposition next turn. This also gives the Necron player a choice weather to teleport all portals in turn one or to save a formation for later turns...
  • The other aspect of the phase out rule is that, given this major weakness, we can design the Necrons as the elite force of powerful units as it is described to be without having to make the formations too small or too expensive. Necron formations will have to use a portal to move back into the reserve which also will be a limiting factor for how the formations are used compared its predecessors. It will also be more in line with how Epic works in general even thou moving back into reserve is a unique ability for the Necrons. With this design we envision the Necron formations to be very powerful at the start of the game, but once they start taking casualties you will have to be careful to avoid a formation phase out.

The basic Necron rules has also been changed. The regeneration/reanimation has been replaced by a 5+ invulnerable save to represent the Necrons abilities to repair themselves. We felt this simplifies gameplay as dead units will always remain dead. Most Necron units also received the Machine Spirit and Expendable special rules to represent their machine origin. While Expendable will make them very hard to break with shooting, the Machine Spirit rule means that formations that lost their Lord will be significantly weaker.

The strategy rating of the Necrons has been changed to a 1 to represent their slow and steady advance. They also received an army rule allowing them to re-roll failed teleport rolls. The intention is that you should arrive at the battlefield in better shape, but you will have to be a bit more conservative with your teleports as your opponent will most likely get a change to act before you do.
